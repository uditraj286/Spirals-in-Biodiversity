Fibonacci Spirals

Discrete Mathematics

Integer sequences creating perfect spiral arrangements in nature

Each number equals the sum of the two preceding numbers

Natural Occurrences:

  • Pinecone scales: 8 spirals in one direction, 13 in the other
  • Pineapple sections: 8, 13, or 21 spiral patterns
  • Flower petals: commonly 3, 5, 8, 13, or 21 petals

Mathematical Formulations

Logarithmic Spiral


r = ae^(bθ)


Where r is the radius, θ is the angle, and a, b are constants. This equation describes the growth pattern of nautilus shells and galaxies.

Golden Spiral


r = φ^(2θ/π)


A special case of the logarithmic spiral where the growth factor equals the golden ratio, commonly found in plant phyllotaxis.

Phyllotactic Spiral


α = 360°/φ²


The golden angle (≈137.5°) between successive elements in plant arrangements, maximizing exposure and packing efficiency.
